My Challenges & Learnings

My Challenges

“As a 3rd year Computer Science student, one of the biggest challenges I face is maintaining motivation for my course. The pandemic deeply affected my learning experience during my earlier years. Instead of gaining practical skills through face-to-face classes, collaboration, and hands-on projects, I spent most of my time learning online in isolation. This made it harder for me to fully connect with the subject and develop the passion and confidence that I had hoped for. The gap in experience has sometimes left me feeling unprepared and discouraged, especially when facing more complex topics and projects in Computer Science. However, I am trying to overcome this by finding ways to reignite my interest—whether through personal projects, joining peer groups, or seeking guidance from mentors. .”

— Lester Ciano.

My Learnings

“As a 3rd year student, I’ve learned that being a student is not all about studying or focusing only on academic achievements. Education comes in many forms, and one of the most important is spiritual growth. Jehovah reminds us in Matthew 6:33-34: “Keep on, then, seeking first the Kingdom and his righteousness, and all these other things will be added to you. So never be anxious about the next day, for the next day will have its own anxieties. Each day has enough of its own troubles.” This has helped me realize that I shouldn’t let myself be overwhelmed by pressure. Instead of pushing myself too hard just to meet expectations, I strive to prioritize what truly matters, trusting that with Jehovah’s guidance, everything else will follow. ”

— Lester Ciano
